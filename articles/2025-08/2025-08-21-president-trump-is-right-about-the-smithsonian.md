-   The National Museum of African American History and Culture [debuted
    a series](https://archive.ph/Y6NLt) to educate people on “a society
    that privileges white people and whiteness” —
    [defining](https://www.theamericanconservative.com/smithsonian-whiteness-anti-white-propaganda/)
    so-called “[white dominant
    culture](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1958252901685772373)“
    as “ways white people and their traditions, attitudes, and ways of
    life have been normalized over time” and
    [portraying](https://www.aei.org/op-eds/smithsonian-institution-explains-that-rationality-hard-work-are-racist/)
    “the nuclear family,” “work ethic,” and “intellect” as white
    qualities rooted in racism.
    -   The campaign featured [content](https://archive.ph/Y6NLt) from
        [hardcore woke
        activist](https://thefederalist.com/2020/08/14/the-woke-war-on-history-how-ibram-x-kendi-and-others-distort-facts-to-fit-narratives/)
        Ibram X. Kendi.

-   As part of its
    [campaign](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/04/27/arts/design/national-portrait-gallery-performances.html)
    to stop being “wealthy, pale, and male,” the National Portrait
    Gallery featured a choreographed “[modern dance
    performance](https://www.si.edu/events/detail?trumbaEmbed=view%3Devent%26eventid%3D158636545)“
    detailing the
    “[ramifications](https://npg.si.edu/about-us/press-release/dana-tai-soon-burgess-dance-company-debut-two-new-performances-its-national)“
    of the southern border wall and [commissioned an entire
    series](https://npg.si.edu/exhibit/identify/index.html) to examine
    “American portraiture and institutional history… through the lens of
    historical exclusion.”
-   The American History Museum prominently displays the
    “Intersex-Inclusive Progress Pride flag” [at its
    entrance](https://thefederalist.com/2025/08/15/smithsonians-american-history-museum-is-wall-to-wall-anti-american-propaganda/#:~:text=At%20the%20American%20History%20Museum%2C%20which%20is%20located%20in%20the%20shadow%20of%20the%20Washington%20Monument%2C%20Americans%20who%20want%20to%20learn%20about%20their%20history%20must%20first%20be%20bombarded%20at%20the%20front%20entrance%20with%20a%20%E2%80%9Cpride%2Dprogress%E2%80%9D%20flag.),
    which was also
    [flown](https://www.cooperhewitt.org/2023/06/20/intersex-inclusive-progress-pride-flag-at-the-smithsonian/)
    alongside the American flag at multiple Smithsonian campuses.

-   The National Museum of African Art
    [displayed](https://www.si.edu/newsdesk/releases/deep-wake-drexciya-ayana-v-jackson-opens-april-29-national-museum-african-art)
    an exhibit on “works of speculative fiction that bring to life an
    immersive, feminist and sacred aquatopia inspired by the legend of
    Drexciya,” an “underwater kingdom populated by the children of
    pregnant women who had been thrown overboard or jumped into the
    ocean during the Middle Passage.”
-   The American History Museum’s “LGBTQ+ History” exhibit
    [seeks](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/topics/lgbtq-history#:~:text=The%20museum%20is,many%20other%20experiences.)
    to “understand evolving and overlapping identities such as lesbian,
    gay, bi-sexual, transgender, queer, transsexual, transvestite, mahu,
    homosexual, fluid, invert, urning, third sex, two sex,
    gender-bender, sapphist, hijra, friend of Dorothy, drag queen/king,
    and many other experiences,” and includes articles on “[LGBTQ+
    inclusion and
    skateboarding](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/stories/place-park-lgbtq-inclusion-and-skateboarding)“
    and “[the rise of drag ball culture in the
    1920s](https://americanhistory.si.edu/explore/stories/queens-and-queers-rise-drag-ball-culture-1920s).”
-   The National Museum of the American Latino features
    [programming](https://latino.si.edu/exhibitions/GAC/thriving-diversity)
    highlighting “animated Latinos and Latinas with disabilities” — with
    [content](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dlp0S0UAA2o&t=20s) from “a
    disabled, plus-sized actress” and an “ambulatory wheelchair user”
    who “educates on their identity being Latinx, LGBTQ+, and disabled.”
