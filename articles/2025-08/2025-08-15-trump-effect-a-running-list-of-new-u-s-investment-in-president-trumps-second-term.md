Since President Donald J. Trump took office, his unwavering commitment
to revitalizing American industry has spurred trillions of dollars of
[investments](https://www.whitehouse.gov/investments/) in U.S.
manufacturing, production, and innovation — and the list only continues
to grow.

**Here is a non-comprehensive running list of new U.S.-based investments
in President Trump’s second term:**

-   **Apple** [announced](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2025/08/apple-increases-us-commitment-to-600-billion-usd-announces-ambitious-program/) a
    $600 billion investment in U.S. manufacturing and workforce training
    as it brings additional components of its supply chain and advanced
    manufacturing back to the U.S. — along with an American
    manufacturing program to incentivize its suppliers to make their
    products in the U.S.
-   **Project Stargate**, led by Japan-based Softbank and U.S.-based
    OpenAI and
    Oracle, [announced](https://openai.com/index/announcing-the-stargate-project/) a
    $500 billion private investment in U.S.-based artificial
    intelligence infrastructure.

    [announced](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nvidia-manufacture-american-made-ai-supercomputers-us/)
    it will invest $500 billion in U.S.-based AI infrastructure over the
    next four years amid its pledge to manufacture AI supercomputers
    entirely in the U.S. for the first time.
