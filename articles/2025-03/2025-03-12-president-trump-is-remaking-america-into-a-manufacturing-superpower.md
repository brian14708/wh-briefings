title: President Trump is Remaking America into a Manufacturing Superpower
---
 
**President Donald J. Trump is on a mission to make America the
manufacturing superpower of the world once again — and in just seven
weeks, he has made incredible progress. **Companies from around the
world are responding with new investments as President Trump levels the
playing field for American workers and businesses.

**Here is a non-comprehensive list of investments secured since
President Trump took office:**

-   Apple [announced](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2025/02/apple-will-spend-more-than-500-billion-usd-in-the-us-over-the-next-four-years/) a
    historic $500 billion investment, which will create 20,000 new
    U.S.-based jobs.
-   President
    Trump [announced](https://www.foxbusiness.com/politics/trump-announces-major-ai-infrastructure-investment) a
    $500 billion private investment in artificial intelligence
    infrastructure — with major
    CEOs [agreeing](https://x.com/baldwin_daniel_/status/1882059615971725796) it
    would not have been possible without President Trump’s leadership.
-   TSMC [announced](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/03/another-historic-investment-secured-under-president-trump/) an
    unprecedented $100 billion investment in U.S.-based semiconductor
    chip manufacturing.
-   Eli Lilly and Company [announced](https://archive.ph/PqRtn) a $27
    billion investment in its U.S.-based manufacturing.
-   President
    Trump [announced](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1897736053504565716) shipping
    giant CMA CGM will invest $20 billion in U.S. shipping and
    logistics, which will create 10,000 new American jobs.
-   President
    Trump [announced](https://dailycaller.com/2025/01/07/trump-announces-20-billion-investment-in-u-s-data-centers/) a
    $20 billion investment by DAMAC Properties to build new U.S.-based
    data centers.
-   Wisconsin-based Clarios, a leader in low-voltage energy
    storage, [announced](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/clarios-announces-6-billion-american-energy-manufacturing-strategy-302389129.html) a
    $6 billion plan to expand its U.S.-based manufacturing.
-   Stellantis [announced](https://apnews.com/article/stellantis-uaw-union-illinois-trump-c61f162889384fc7e1337dd5613692d6) a
    $5 billion investment to reopen its Belvidere, Illinois, assembly
    plant — which will put 1,500 employees back to work — and
    make [improvements](https://www.freep.com/story/money/cars/chrysler/2025/01/22/stellantis-belvidere-detroit-plant-new-midsize-truck-dodge-durango/77876749007/) across
    its U.S.-based manufacturing network.
-   GE
    Aerospace [announced](https://www.geaerospace.com/news/press-releases/ge-aerospace-invest-nearly-1b-us-manufacturing-2025) a
    $1 billion investment in its manufacturing operations across 16
    states — creating 5,000 new jobs.
-   Merck [announced](https://www.reuters.com/business/healthcare-pharmaceuticals/merck-opens-vaccine-manufacturing-facility-north-carolina-2025-03-11/) it
    will invest $8 billion in the U.S. over the next several years after
    opening its new $1 billion North Carolina manufacturing facility.
-   GE
    Vernova [announced](https://www.gevernova.com/news/press-releases/ge-vernova-invest-almost-600-million-us-factories-facilities-over-next-two-years) it
    will invest nearly $600 million in its U.S.-based factories over the
    next two years, which will create more than 1,500 new jobs.
-   Diageo North
    America [announced](https://www.diageo.com/en/news-and-media/press-releases/2025/diageo-north-america-announces-new-manufacturing-and-warehousing-facility-in-alabama) a
    $415 million investment in a new Alabama manufacturing facility.
-   Eaton [announced](https://www.eaton.com/us/en-us/company/news-insights/news-releases/2025/eaton-invests-in-new-south-carolina-transformer-manufacturing.html) a
    $340 million investment in a new South Carolina-based manufacturing
    facility for its three-phase transformers.
-   Siemens [announced](https://apnews.com/article/siemens-3256320c6977e21c679b2286ca45d694) a
    $285 million investment in U.S. electrical product manufacturing,
    which will create more than 900 new skilled manufacturing jobs.
-   Switzerland-based
    ABB [announced](https://mailchi.mp/mail.whitehouse.gov/manufacturingdive.com/news/swiss-giant-abb-invest-120-million-us-manufacturing/741971/) a
    $120 million investment to expand production of its low-voltage
    electrification products in Tennessee and Mississippi.
-   Saica Group, a Spain-based corrugated packaging
    maker, [announced](https://archive.ph/JMsiO) [plans](https://www.ibj.com/articles/european-packaging-company-to-build-110m-plant-in-anderson) to
    build a $110 million new manufacturing facility in Anderson,
    Indiana.
-   Saint Gobain
    Ceramics [announced](https://www.csrwire.com/press_releases/818561-saint-gobain-ceramics-build-new-norpro-manufacturing-facility-wheatfield-new) a
    new $40 million NorPro manufacturing facility in Wheatfield, New
    York.
-   Asahi Group Holdings, one of the largest Japanese beverage
    makers, [announced](https://www.wsj.com/business/japan-beer-maker-asahi-looks-to-invest-more-in-the-u-s-as-president-trump-wields-tariffs-c23eaab1?mod=latest_headlines) a
    $35 million investment to boost production at its Wisconsin plant.
-   LGM
    Pharma [announced](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250312785272/en/LGM-Pharma-Invests-6M-in-U.S.-Drug-Manufacturing-Capabilities-for-Liquids-Suspensions-Semi-Solids-and-Suppositories) a
    $6 million investment to expand its manufacturing facility in
    Rosenberg, Texas.
-   ViDARR Inc., a defense optical equipment
    manufacturer, [announced](https://www.governor.virginia.gov/newsroom/news-releases/2025/march/name-1042428-en.html) a
    $2.69 million investment to open a new facility in Virginia.

In addition to the private investment, Saudi
Arabia [announced](https://apnews.com/article/saudi-arabia-us-investment-trump-6730a89f93b44ed8d705638f95700cbb) it
intends to invest $600 billion in the U.S. over the next four years,
United Arab
Emirates [announced](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/global-companies-eye-more-us-222041213.html#:~:text=United%20Arab%20Emirates%20energy%20company%20ADNOC%20on%20Tuesday%20said%20it%20intended%20to%20make%20significant%20investments%20in%20the%20coming%20months%20in%20the%20U.S.%20through%20its%20fully%2Downed%20international%20investment%20arm%20XRG%2C%20which%20has%20about%20%2480%20billion%20in%20assets%20and%20is%20considering%20options%20for%20an%20initial%20public%20offering.) it
will make “significant investments” in the U.S., and
Taiwan [announced](https://www.ft.com/content/bdb5381f-786e-404b-9b24-8735bdc95265) a
pledge to boost its U.S.-based investment.

Meanwhile, to avoid tariffs, electronics giants Samsung and LG
“are [considering](https://www.kedglobal.com/business-politics/newsView/ked202501210009) moving
their plants in Mexico to the U.S.,” Hyundai Motor
is [planning](https://www.reuters.com/business/companies-eye-us-expansion-lessen-fallout-potential-tariffs-2025-01-29/#:~:text=The%20South%20Korean%20automaker%20said%20on%20Jan.%2023%20it%20planned%20to%20further%20localize%20production%20in%20the%20U.S.%20to%20minimize%20any%20tariff%20impact.%20It%20also%20said%20it%20would%20make%20hybrid%20vehicles%20at%20its%20new%20factory%20in%20Georgia.) to
“localize production in the U.S,” Nissan
is [considering](https://www.foxbusiness.com/politics/top-automaker-could-move-some-production-out-mexico-amid-trump-tariff-talks-ceo-says) moving
production from Mexico to the U.S., and Honda
is [expected](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/honda-produce-next-civic-indiana-not-mexico-due-us-tariffs-sources-say-2025-03-03/) to
produce its next-generation Civic hybrid model in Indiana — among [many
other](https://www.reuters.com/business/companies-eye-us-expansion-lessen-fallout-potential-tariffs-2025-01-29/) international
companies contemplating such a move.
