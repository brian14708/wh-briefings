### **FACT: Voter ID laws have no measurable negative effect on turnout.**

-   Following Georgia’s 2021 voter ID law, the state achieved [record
    turnout](https://nationaltoday.com/us/ga/atlanta/news/2026/02/10/georgias-voting-reforms-proved-successful-boosting-turnout/)
    in both the 2022 and 2024 elections.
-   A [2021
    review](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w25522/w25522.pdf)
    by the National Bureau of Economic Research, analyzing a decade of
    data, determined voter ID laws “have no negative effect on
    registration or turnout, overall or for any group defined by race,
    gender, age, or party affiliation.”
-   A [2014
    study](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1089/elj.2013.0209)
    in the *Election Law Journal* found “little support for the
    hypothesis that notification of ID requirements depresses turnout.”
-   A [2023
    report](https://www.americafirstpolicy.com/issues/research-report-the-impacts-of-photo-identification-requirements-on-voting)
    by the America First Policy Institute stated voter ID laws are “not
    a significant cause for decreased turnout” and “do not negatively
    affect minority or demographic-specific turnout,” noting that
    several states (and [entire
    countries](https://www.americafirstpolicy.com/issues/research-report-the-impacts-of-photo-identification-requirements-on-voting#:~:text=Excluding%20countries%20that%20actively%20mandate%20voter%20participation%2C%20the%20median%20voter%20turnout%20is%2066.2%25%20(International%20IDEA%2C%20n.d.)%2C%20and%20the%20highest%20turnout%20is%2084.2%25.%20These%20are%20strong%20numbers%20compared%20to%20the%20U.S.%20median%20turnout%20of%2046.6%25%20in%20the%202022%20midterms%20and%2066.6%25%20in%20the%202020%20general%20election.))
    with photo ID laws actually had higher turnout than those without.
-   A [2009
    study](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/ps-political-science-and-politics/article/empirical-effects-of-voterid-laws-present-orabsent/00F78D1DA0DFB2DC842D499DD226A196)
    in *PS: Political Science & Politics* concluded voter ID laws “have
    not had a significant impact on voting” and show “little to no
    effect on aggregate or individual-level turnout.”
-   A [2006
    study](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=925611)
    by the Crime Prevention Research Center found “\[i\]t is hard to see
    any evidence that voting regulations differentially harm either
    minorities, the elderly, or the poor,” and that “\[r\]egulations
    that prevent fraud are shown to actually increase the voter
    participation rate.”
-   A [2007
    analysis](https://www.heritage.org/election-integrity/report/new-analysis-shows-voter-identification-laws-do-not-reduce-turnout)
    by the Heritage Foundation found that “voter identification
    requirements, such as requiring non-photo and photo identification,
    have virtually no suppressive effect on reported voter turnout.”

### **FACT: Voting by mail increases the risk of fraud.**

-   The [Commission on Federal Election
    Reform](https://web.archive.org/web/20200419202148/https:/legislationline.org/download/id/1472/file/3b50795b2d0374cbef5c29766256.pdf)
    warned that voting by mail is “likely to increase the risks of fraud
    and of contested elections,” that “absentee ballots remain the
    largest source of potential voter fraud,” and that “vote buying
    schemes are far more difficult to detect when citizens vote by
    mail.”
-   A [2012 *New York Times*
    investigation](https://www.nytimes.com/2012/10/07/us/politics/as-more-vote-by-mail-faulty-ballots-could-impact-elections.html#:~:text=Yet%20votes%20cast%20by%20mail%20are%20less%20likely%20to%20be%20counted%2C%20more%20likely%20to%20be%20compromised%20and%20more%20likely%20to%20be%20contested%20than%20those%20cast%20in%20a%20voting%20booth%2C%20statistics%20show.%20Election%20officials%20reject%20almost%202%20percent%20of%20ballots%20cast%20by%20mail%2C%20double%20the%20rate%20for%20in%2Dperson%20voting.)
    revealed that “votes cast by mail are less likely to be counted,
    more likely to be compromised and more likely to be contested than
    those cast in a voting booth.”
-   In 2024, the House Republican Policy Committee
    [found](https://republicanpolicy.house.gov/sites/evo-subsites/republicanpolicy.house.gov/files/evo-media-document/Dangers%20of%20Mass%20Mail-In%20Voting%20-%20Final.pdf)
    that mass mail-in voting “increases opportunities for error in fraud
    in states that do not maintain clean voter rolls” and “brings about
    an inherent set of risks that jeopardize the integrity of American
    elections and create absurdities that dampen faith in our electoral
    system.”
-   [MIT Election Data + Science
    Lab](https://electionlab.mit.edu/research/voting-mail-and-absentee-voting#:~:text=However%2C%20even%20many%20scholars%20who%20argue%20that%20fraud%20is%20generally%20rare%20agree%20that%20fraud%20with%20VBM%20voting%20seems%20to%20be%20more%20frequent%20than%20with%20in%2Dperson%20voting.):
    “\[E\]ven many scholars who argue that fraud is generally rare agree
    that fraud with \[voting by mail\] voting seems to be more frequent
    than with in-person voting.”
-   A 2023 Heartland Institute/Rasmussen poll
    [found](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/heartland-rasmussen-poll-one-five-161100197.html?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAJbZs8elb6GMCLjC15uMUtqR1b-C5xruLe6z7YvGydqnR6xl9DJzGPGDO4Qso2co4Za_NNjKOB0GJeMtn70xSq_qAdhDhebuwjlRgJsVNvv-uly9jHsk6rGV1Tl9UCm-oprRCZZNWDZ0Wiqa7MWKfSF7uxfAdzKKNoc6qb6Y0Bjo)
    that 21% of mail-in-voters admitted to at least one form of voter
    fraud in the 2020 election.
-   A [2020
    report](https://www.heritage.org/sites/default/files/2020-07/LM268_0.pdf)
    by the Heritage Foundation
    [described](https://www.heritage.org/election-integrity/report/four-stolen-elections-the-vulnerabilities-absentee-and-mail-ballots#:~:text=All%2050%20states,mail%2Din%20voting.)
    widespread absentee and early voting as “vulnerable to being
    misdirected, stolen, forged, and altered,” with voters susceptible
    to intimidation, unlawful assistance, or pressure from campaigns and

-   In 2008, the U.S. Supreme Court,
