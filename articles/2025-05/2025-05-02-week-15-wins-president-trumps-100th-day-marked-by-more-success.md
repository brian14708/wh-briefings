title: "WEEK 15 WINS: President Trump\u2019s 100th Day Marked by More Success"
---
 
This week, President Donald J. Trump celebrated his 100th day in office
— and set the course for the next 100 days of growth, prosperity, and
success for the American people.  
  
**Here is a non-comprehensive list of wins in week 15:**

-   The economy
    [added](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/05/jobs-boom-more-americans-working-for-higher-pay/)
    177,000 new jobs in April, according to the latest jobs report —
    smashing expectations for another month as the workforce grows and
    businesses onshore jobs.
-   President Donald J. Trump’s relentless pursuit of manufacturing
    dominance spurred onshoring and additional U.S. investment.
    -   Mercedes-Benz [announced](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/01/mercedes-benz-alabama-plant-trump-tariffs.html) it
        will move production of another vehicle to its Tuscaloosa,
        Alabama, manufacturing facility.
    -   AstraZeneca
        [announced](https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/apr/29/astrazeneca-production-us-trump-tariffs-uk-europe-pascal-soriot)
        it will shift production of some medicines from Europe to the
        U.S.
    -   Walmart
        [expanded](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1917208431724765418)
        its support for American-made products.
    -   IBM [announced ](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2025-04-28-ibm-unveils-150-billion-investment-in-america-to-accelerate-technology-opportunity)a
        $150 billion investment over the next five years in its
        U.S.-based growth and manufacturing operations.
    -   Pratt Industries
        [announced](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1917573559851049137)
        a $5 billion investment that will result in 5,000 new
        manufacturing jobs across several key industrial states.
    -   Kimberly-Clark
        [announced](https://www.wsj.com/business/kimberly-clark-kleenex-investing-2-billion-manufacturing-us-503c4c7d)
        a $2 billion investment in its U.S. manufacturing sites, which
        will create 900 new jobs.
    -   Corning [announced](https://www.corning.com/worldwide/en/about-us/news-events/news-releases/2025/04/corning-expands-advanced-manufacturing-capacity-to-meet-increased-demand-for-US-made-solar-products.html) it
        is expanding its Michigan manufacturing facility investment to
        $1.5 billion.
    -   Merck &
        Co. [announced](https://www.merck.com/news/merck-breaks-ground-on-new-1-billion-biologics-center-of-excellence-in-wilmington-delaware/) a
        $1 billion investment to build a new state-of-the-art biologics
        manufacturing plant in Delaware, which will create at least 500
        new jobs — part of the
        company’s [commitment](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1917209821889130675) to
        invest more than $9 billion over the next four years.
        -   “Since the advent of the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, Merck
            has allocated more than $12 billion to enhance our domestic
            manufacturing and research capabilities, with additional
            planned investments of more than $9 billion over the next
            four years.”
    -   Amgen [announced](https://www.amgen.com/newsroom/press-releases/2025/04/amgen-announces-900-million-manufacturing-expansion-creation-of-350-new-jobs-in-ohio) a
        $900 million investment in its Ohio-based manufacturing
        operation.
        -   “Pro-growth policies like the @POTUS @WhiteHouse 2017 Tax
            Cuts and Jobs Act helped make investments like this
            possible. Since enactment, Amgen has invested ~$5B in
            capital expenditures. This amounts to an additional
            downstream output to the U.S. economy of approximately
            $12B.”
    -   The Bel
        Group [announced](https://belbrandsusa.com/news/bel-strengthens-its-industrial-footprint-with-more-than-350-million-investment-in-the-u-s/) a
        $350 million investment to expand its U.S.-based production,
        including at its South Dakota, Idaho and Wisconsin facilities —
        which will create 250 new jobs.
-   President Trump continued to secure our border and rid our
    communities of illegal immigrant criminals.
    -   [*New York
        Post*](https://nypost.com/2025/04/30/us-news/illegal-border-crossings-remained-near-historic-lows-in-april-after-trumps-crackdown/):
        Illegal border crossings remained near historic lows in April
        after President Trump’s crackdown
    -   The Trump Administration
        [directed](https://www.foxnews.com/us/more-than-100-illegal-immigrants-arrested-colorado-springs-massive-underground-nightclub-raid)
        an operation at an underground nightclub in Colorado “frequented
        by TdA and MS-13 terrorists” that resulted in 100 illegal
        immigrant arrests.
    -   ICE
        [arrested](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/05/01/us/politics/ice-arrest-immigration-florida.html)
        more than 1,000 illegal immigrants in Florida in just six days
        as part of Operation Tidal Wave.
    -   Uzbekistan
        [agreed](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/immigrants-return-uzbekistan)
        to pay for and accept 131 illegal immigrants from Uzbekistan,
        Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan.
-   President Trump continued to pursue peace through strength around
    the world.
    -   President Trump
        [secured](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/05/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-secures-agreement-to-establish-united-states-ukraine-reconstruction-investment-fund/)
        a historic agreement with Ukraine that gives the U.S. an
        economic stake in securing a free, peaceful, and sovereign
        future for Ukraine and allows for the long-term reconstruction
        and modernization of the country after Russia’s invasion.
    -   President Trump
        [announced](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/114433959082847679)
        secondary sanctions on any country or person who purchases
        Iranian oil.
    -   President Trump secured the release of a [wrongfully
        detained](https://x.com/SecRubio/status/1917610906944499821)
        U.S. citizen in Belarus and a U.S. citizen
        [imprisoned](https://x.com/SebGorka/status/1917752015624585688)
        in Kuwait — for a total of 47 detained citizens abroad
        [freed](https://x.com/SecRubio/status/1917610906944499821) since
        President Trump took office.
    -   The Trump Administration
        [brokered](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/africa/rwanda-and-dr-congo-sign-joint-pledge-toward-peace-deal-by-may-2/3549559)
        a joint pledge for peace between Rwanda and the Democratic
        Republic of the Congo.
    -   The Department of the Treasury [cracked
        down](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0113) on
        vessels delivering oil derivatives to Houthi terrorists in
        Yemen.
    -   The Department of the Treasury
        [sanctioned](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0116)
        six Iranian and Chinese firms linked to procuring missile
        propellant ingredients for the Iranian regime.
-   The Trump Administration forged ahead on its unprecedented effort to
    secure American energy dominance.
    -   Woodside Energy Group [financially
        approved](https://www.woodside.com/media-centre/news-stories/story/woodside-approves-louisiana-lng-development)
        a $17.5 billion liquefied natural gas (LNG) project.
    -   The Environmental Protection Agency
        [granted](https://abcnews.go.com/US/wireStory/epa-allowing-sale-cheaper-higher-ethanol-e15-gasoline-121251004)
        an emergency waiver that allows Americans to buy cheaper,
        higher-ethanol gasoline through the summer, which will save
        Americans money.
-   President Trump took a series of executive actions to improve
    Americans’ lives.
    -   President Trump
        [strengthened](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strengthens-americas-law-enforcement-to-pursue-criminals-and-protect-innocent-citizens/)
        the ability of state and local law enforcement to pursue
        criminals and protect innocent Americans.
    -   President Trump signed an [executive
        order](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/protecting-american-communities-from-criminal-aliens/)
        to protect Americans in so-called “sanctuary” jurisdictions from
        dangerous criminal illegal immigrants.
    -   President Trump
        [established](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/05/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-establishes-the-religious-liberty-commission/)
        the Religious Liberty Commission to safeguard and promote
        America’s founding principle of religious freedom.
    -   President Trump
        [incentivized](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-incentivizes-domestic-automobile-production/)
        American automobile production.
    -   President Trump
        [ordered](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-enforces-commonsense-rules-of-the-road-for-americas-truck-drivers/)
        that commercial truck drivers must be properly qualified and
        proficient in English.
    -   President Trump
        [ended](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/05/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-ends-the-taxpayer-subsidization-of-biased-media/)
        the taxpayer subsidization of NPR and PBS.
-   President Trump
    [unveiled](https://www.whitehouse.gov/omb/information-resources/budget/the-presidents-fy-2026-discretionary-budget-request/)
    his proposed budget, which would save taxpayers $163 billion in
    wasteful spending, gut the weaponized deep state, and provide
    historic increases for defense and border security.
-   President Trump
    [launched](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/114417332210818903)
    the FEMA Review Council to help fix the broken disaster response
    system and return power to the states.
-   President Trump
    [announced](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1917317061245591969)
    Selfridge Air National Guard Base in Michigan will soon be home to
    the new F-15EW Eagle II fighter jets.
-   President Trump
    [renamed](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/114435902192931017)
    May 8 as “Victory Day for World War II” and November 11 as “Victory
    Day for World War I” in recognition of America’s role in winning the
    two wars.
-   The Department of Health and Human services
    [released](https://www.hhs.gov/press-room/gender-dysphoria-report-release.html)
    a comprehensive review of so-called “gender-affirming care,”
    [finding](https://www.nationalreview.com/2025/05/hhs-report-finds-no-strong-evidence-supporting-gender-affirming-care-effectiveness/)
    no strong medical or scientific evidence exists to support the
    treatment’s irreversible effects.
-   The Trump Administration
    [ended](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1917572798857281790)
    the Biden-era lawfare against South Dakota cattle ranchers who were
    wrongfully persecuted over a minor land dispute.
-   The Department of State
    [designated](https://www.state.gov/releases/office-of-the-spokesperson/2025/05/terrorist-designations-of-viv-ansanm-and-gran-grif/)
    Haitian gangs Viv Ansanm and Gran Grif as Foreign Terrorist
    Organizations.
-   The Department of Education
    [launched](https://x.com/EDSecMcMahon/status/1915873761447841965) a
    civil rights investigation into the New York Department of Education
    over its threat to withhold funding from the Massapequa School
    District if it does not eliminate its Native American mascot.
-   The Department of Education
    [announced](https://www.ed.gov/about/news/press-release/us-department-of-educations-office-civil-rights-finds-university-of-pennsylvania-has-violated-title-ix) its
    finding that the University of Pennsylvania violated Title IX,
    notifying the institution that they have ten days to resolve the
    violations or risk a referral to the Department of Justice for
    enforcement proceedings.
-   The Department of Education and the Department of Health and Human
    Services
    [announced](https://www.ed.gov/about/news/press-release/ed-hhs-launch-title-vi-investigations-of-harvard-university-and-harvard-law-review-amid-allegations-of-discriminatory-practices) investigations
    into Harvard University and the Harvard Law Review based on reports
    of race-based discrimination permeating the operations of the
    journal.
-   The Department of the Interior
    [announced](https://www.doi.gov/pressreleases/department-interior-announces-expansion-hunting-and-fishing-opportunities)
    42 new proposed hunting opportunities across 87,000 acres within the
    National Wildlife Refuge System and National Fish Hatchery System,
    which would more than triple the number of opportunities and
    quintuple the number of stations opened or expanded compared to the
    previous administration.
-   The Department of Energy
    [announced](https://www.energy.gov/articles/energy-department-lifts-regulations-miscellaneous-gas-products)
    it will lift a range of unnecessary regulations on certain indoor
    and outdoor gas products — expanding choice and lowering costs for
    consumers.
-   The Department of Transportation
    [unveiled](https://www.transportation.gov/briefing-room/us-transportation-secretary-sean-p-duffy-unveils-new-package-boost-air-traffic)
    a new package of actions to further supercharge the air traffic
    controller workforce.
-   Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard
    [added](https://washingtonreporter.news/p/exclusive-tulsi-gabbard-adds-counter)
    counter narcotics to the National Counter Terrorism Center in order
    to “focus intelligence and vetting resources against these
    terrorists who traffic deadly narcotics into the country.”
-   The Department of Justice
    [arrested](https://x.com/FBIDirectorKash/status/1917641945318580661)
    two individuals on charges of operating an international child
    exploitation enterprise.
-   The Department of Agriculture
    [secured](https://x.com/SecRollins/status/1916948485573603627) an
    agreement with Mexico for an immediate transfer of water from
    international reservoirs to Texas farmers and ranchers.
-   The White House Council on Environmental Quality
    [established](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/04/the-white-house-council-on-environmental-quality-establishes-permitting-innovation-center/)
    the Permitting Innovation Center to cut red tape and accelerate the
    environmental review process.
-   The National Institutes of Health
    [announced](https://x.com/NIH/status/1917601482364403734) it will
    publish studies it funds online for free to empower Americans’ own
    research and promote maximum transparency.
-   PepsiCo
    [announced](https://www.aol.com/pepsico-remove-artificial-ingredients-popular-174749466.html)
    it will remove artificial ingredients from some popular food
    offerings by the end of the year following the Trump
    Administration’s push to end artificial food dyes.
