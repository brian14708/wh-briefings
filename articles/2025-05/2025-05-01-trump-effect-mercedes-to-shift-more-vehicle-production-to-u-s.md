title: 'TRUMP EFFECT: Mercedes to Shift More Vehicle Production to U.S.'
---
 
Today, Mercedes-Benz
[announced](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/01/mercedes-benz-alabama-plant-trump-tariffs.html)
it will move production of another vehicle to the U.S. — the latest
result of President Donald J. Trump’s relentless pursuit of American
manufacturing dominance.

The automaker will produce the vehicle at its Tuscaloosa, Alabama, plant
following
[signals](https://www.foxbusiness.com/markets/mercedes-benz-ceo-signals-potential-more-us-investment)
earlier this year that the company will make additional investment in
its U.S.-based operations.

**Mercedes isn’t the only automaker onshoring production as President
Trump incentivizes making things in America again:**

-   BMW
    is [considering ](https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2025/04/10/bmw-production-boost-us-plant/83034910007/)adding
    shifts to boost production at its South Carolina plant.
-   Honda
    [plans](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-04-16/honda-to-shift-production-of-civic-from-japan-to-us-on-tariffs) to
    shift production of the Civic from Japan to the U.S.
-   Hyundai [announced](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/03/24/south-korea-hyundai-us-investment.html) a
    $20 billion investment — including $5.8 billion for a [new Louisiana
    steel
    plant](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/1904240895488078062) to
    support its U.S.-based vehicle production — amid
    their [pledge](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/hyundai-motor-q4-profit-falls-17-discounts-weigh-2025-01-23/) to
    “further localize production in the U.S.”
-   Kia
    [plans](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/kia-produce-hybrid-cars-hyundais-georgia-factory-next-year-2025-03-28/)
    to produce hybrid vehicles at its affiliate Hyundai’s Georgia
    factory.
-   Nissan
    is [considering](https://www.foxbusiness.com/politics/top-automaker-could-move-some-production-out-mexico-amid-trump-tariff-talks-ceo-says) moving
    production from Mexico to the U.S.
-   Stellantis [announced](https://apnews.com/article/stellantis-uaw-union-illinois-trump-c61f162889384fc7e1337dd5613692d6) it
    will reopen its Belvidere, Illinois, plant to build a new midsize
    pickup truck.
-   Toyota
    [announced](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-04-23/toyota-invests-88-million-in-w-virginia-plant-for-hybrid-output)
    it will boost hybrid vehicle production at its West Virginia plant.
