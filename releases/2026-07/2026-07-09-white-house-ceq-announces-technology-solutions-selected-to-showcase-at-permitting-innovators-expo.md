*Industry partners to showcase permitting technologies at the Permitting
Innovators Expo.*

Today, the Council on Environmental Quality (CEQ) announced the
innovators invited to showcase their technology solutions at the
inaugural [Permitting Innovators
Expo](https://www.permittinginnovators.com/) on July 31 in the
Washington, D.C. area.

Following President Trump’s directive to <a
href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/updating-permitting-technology-for-the-21st-century/"
id="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/updating-permitting-technology-for-the-21st-century/">update
permitting technology for the 21st century</a>, CEQ invited industry
partners to submit technology solutions that could accelerate and
modernize federal environmental reviews and permitting. A panel of
professionals with relevant experience reviewed the pool of submissions
using CEQ’s
<a href="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/evaluation-criteria/"
id="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/evaluation-criteria/">evaluation
criteria</a>, considering whether the solutions have the potential to
address key technology gaps outlined in the
<a href="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/"
id="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/">Permitting
Technology Action Plan</a>. CEQ reviewed the evaluations prior to making
final selections.

The selected technology solutions represent a range of approaches to
improving federal permitting, including tools that support document
analysis, workflow management, data access and integration, and routine
task automation.

The innovators will demonstrate their technology solutions at the
Permitting Innovators Expo on July 31 to federal agencies and industry
leaders working to modernize environmental review and permitting. Anyone
interested in attending the Permitting Innovators Expo may
<a href="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/"
id="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/">submit an interest
form</a>.

CEQ will also highlight technology solutions in the Permitting
Innovators Solutions Catalog, which will be shared with federal agencies
in late 2026.
