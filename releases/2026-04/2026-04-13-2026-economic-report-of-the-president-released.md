The *Economic Report of the President* is prepared annually by the
Council of Economic Advisers, providing a comprehensive review of the
past year’s economic policies and their impact on current and future
economic conditions. This year’s *Report* breaks down 14 topics of
importance to the economy and to American families.

Each of the *Report*’s chapters delves deeply into one of the
Administration’s policy priorities.

1.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 1: The Economic
    and Fiscal Benefits of the One Big Beautiful Bill
    Act](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-1.-The-Economic-and-Fiscal-Benefits-of-the-One-Big-Beautiful-Bill-Act.pdf)</span>
    provides estimates for the economic and fiscal impact of the OBBBA,
    including on GDP, wages, and debt;
2.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 2: Promoting
    Prosperity through Regulatory
    Reform](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-2.-Promoting-Prosperity-through-Regulatory-Reform.pdf)</span>
    measures the cost of regulations and estimates the savings from
    deregulatory reforms undertaken by the Trump Administration;
3.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 3: Rebuilding
    America’s International Trade
    Policy](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-3.-Rebuilding-Americas-International-Trade-Policy.pdf)</span>
    articulates the economic framework underpinning the Trump
    Administration’s America First trade policy and its early successes;
4.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 4: Achieving
    Energy Dominance to Power American
    Prosperity](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-4.-Achieving-Energy-Dominance-to-Power-American-Prosperity.pdf)</span>
    shows how unleashing American energy will boost growth and enhance
    national and economic security;  
5.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 5: The Revolution
    of Artificial
    Intelligence](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-5.-The-Revolution-of-Artificial-Intelligence.pdf)</span>
    catalogs the rapid development of AI and America’s global
    leadership;
6.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 6: Protecting and
    Rebuilding the American Dream of
    Homeownership](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-6.-Protecting-and-Rebuilding-the-American-Dream-of-Homeownership.pdf)</span>
    assesses the scale, causes, and consequences of the housing crunch
    and quantifies the potential affordability and growth effects of
    implementing state and local best practices to unleash housing
    supply;
7.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 7: Strengthening
    America’s Industrial Supply
    Chains](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-7.-Strengthening-Americas-Industrial-Supply-Chains.pdf)</span>
    outlines a systematic framework for assessing supply chain
    vulnerabilities and understanding the steps the Administration is
    taking to remedy them and create greater industrial resilience;
8.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 8: Strengthening
    the United States’ Defense Industrial
    Base](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-8.-Strengthening-the-United-States-Defense-Industrial-Base.pdf)</span>
    assesses America’s defense industrial base in a historical and
    international context and discusses the Administration’s solutions
    to further revive the defense industrial base in the face of
    multifaceted demand-side and supply-side barriers;
9.  <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 9: Work Means More
    than Making a
    Living](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-9.-Work-Means-More-Than-Making-a-Living_Labor-Challenges-and-Opportunity.pdf)</span>
    evaluates the role of work in human flourishing along with current
    and prospective Trump Administration actions to elevate workers
    through reforms to the safety net, workforce training, childcare,
    and the tax code;
10. <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 10: the Economic
    Consequences of
    DEI](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-10.-The-Economic-Consequences-of-DEI.pdf)</span>
    quantifies the GDP cost of distortive, non-merit-based, DEI hiring;
11. <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 11: Making America
    Healthy by Unleashing Competition in Physicians’
    Markets](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-11.-Making-America-Healthy-by-Unleashing-Competition-in-Physicians-Markets.pdf)</span>
    looks at Trump Administration’s achievements that improve Americans’
    access to healthcare providers;
12. <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 12: Unlocking
    Retail Access to Private Equity Investments through Defined
    Contribution
    Plans](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-12.-Unlocking-Retail-Access-to-Private-Equity-Investments-through-Defined-Contribution-Plans.pdf)</span>
    uncovers the individual and economic benefits of allowing Americans
    to allocate some of their 401(k)s to private market investments
    previously accessible primarily only to the wealthy;
13. <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 13: The Cost of
    Capital Misallocation to ESG Investments with an Environmental
    Focus](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-13.-The-Cost-of-Capital-Misallocation-to-ESG-Investments-with-an-Environmental-Focus.pdf)</span>
    quantifies the loss to GDP from environmentally-focused capital
    misallocation; and
14. <span style="text-decoration: underline">[Chapter 14: The Year in
    Review and the Years
    Ahead](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ERP-2026-14.-The-Year-in-Review-and-the-Years-Ahead.pdf)</span>
    explains the macroeconomic events of this past year and provides a
    forecast for the years to come under the Administration’s proposed
    policies.

**About the Council of Economic Advisers**

The Council of Economic Advisers was established by the Employment Act
of 1946 to offer the President objective economic advice on the
formulation of both domestic and international economic policy. The
Council bases its recommendations and analysis on economic research and
empirical evidence, using the best data available to support the
President in setting our nation’s economic policy to promote employment,
production, and purchasing power under free competitive enterprise. Its
members are appointed by the President and its chairman is confirmed by
the Senate.
