**The housing market shows clear progress on supply and affordability.**

- New residential construction
  [surged](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/us-housing-starts-surge-to-highest-level-since-december-2024/)
  last month to its highest level in more than a year, reflecting strong
  momentum in expanding housing supply.
- Home prices have
  [declined](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/real-estate/article/home-price-growth-is-slowing-down--and-even-turning-negative--in-more-of-the-country-141505810.html#:~:text=Home%20prices%20declined%20on%20an%20annual%20basis%20in%20more%20than%20half%20the%20country%2C%20spreading%20beyond%20a%20handful%20of%20Sun%20Belt%20cities%20where%20robust%20new%20construction%20activity%20has%20increased%20inventory%20and%20sent%20prices%20lower.)
  on an annual basis in more than half the country, reversing years of
  rapid increases and improving affordability for families and
  first-time buyers.
- Rent growth
  [slowed](https://www.zillow.com/research/march-2026-rent-report-36269/#:~:text=The%20typical%20asking%20rent%20rose%20just%201.8%25%20year%20over%20year%20to%20%241%2C910%2C%20the%20slowest%20annual%20pace%20since%202020.%20Single%2Dfamily%20rents%20climbed%202.5%25%20year%20over%20year%20to%20%242%2C225%2C%20the%20slowest%20annual%20growth%20ever%20recorded%20in%20Zillow%E2%80%99s%20data%20series%2C%20while%20multifamily%20rents%20rose%201.3%25%20to%20%241%2C757.%C2%A0)
  to its lowest pace since President Trump’s first term, with
  single-family rent growth hitting a record low.
- Income growth is now
  [outpacing](https://www.zillow.com/research/march-2026-rent-report-36269/)
  rent growth nationwide,
  [returning](https://www.zillow.com/research/march-2026-rent-report-36269/#:~:text=The%20share%20of%20income%20the%20median%20household%20spends%20on%20typical%20rent%20has%20eased%20to%2026.5%25%2C%20down%20slightly%20from%20a%20year%20ago%20and%20nearing%20the%20pre%2Dpandemic%20level%20of%2025.8%25)
  the typical share of income spent on rent close to pre-pandemic
  levels.

**President Trump’s pro-growth agenda is fueling a manufacturing
resurgence.**

- Orders for business equipment
  [posted](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/us-core-capital-goods-orders-surged-in-march-by-most-since-2020)
  their largest surge in more than six years, while shipments of
  machinery and equipment also
  [shattered](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/us-core-capital-goods-orders-surged-in-march-by-most-since-2020?srnd=homepage-americas#:~:text=The%20durables%20report%20showed%20core%20capital%20goods%20shipments%2C%20a%20less%20volatile%20metric%20that%20excludes%20planes%20and%20military%20hardware%2C%20rose%201.2%25%20after%20an%20upwardly%20revised%201.3%25%20advance%20a%20month%20earlier.)
  [expectations](https://x.com/KushDesai47/status/2049480549446681051) —
  strong signals of expanding industrial capacity.
- The Richmond Fed manufacturing survey
  [jumped](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2049153606545297843) to
  its highest reading in years, joining similarly strong performances
  from the
  [Philadelphia](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2044813942103400877)
  and [New
  York](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4575170-empire-state-manufacturing-index-unexpectedly-shows-positive-reading-in-april)
  Feds, pointing to a broad-based pickup in factory activity across the
  nation.

**Strong consumer confidence and a robust labor market are driving
continued momentum.**

- Consumer confidence
  [rose](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-31/us-consumer-confidence-unexpectedly-rises-on-better-labor-views)
  sharply in March, crushing economists’ expectations as Americans grow
  more optimistic about business and labor market conditions.
- Personal income
  [doubled](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2049837938616250572)
  expectations in March, rising at its fastest rate in nearly a year,
  while personal spending remained robust.
- U.S. economic growth
  [accelerated](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/us-gdp-rose-2-in-early-2026-in-sign-of-economy-s-resilience)
  in the first quarter, powered by the strongest expansion in business
  investment since 2023 and steady consumer demand.
- Initial jobless claims
  [plunged](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/us-jobless-claims-plunge-to-189-000-lowest-since-1969)
  last week to their lowest level since 1969, highlighting the
  exceptional strength of the labor market.

**America is building, investing, and growing once again — because
President Trump has put American jobs, American manufacturing, and
American prosperity first.**
