---
 
**Since returning to office, President Donald J. Trump has unleashed a
bold America First agenda that is crushing bureaucracy, restoring
fairness, and rebuilding American dominance.** Through sweeping
deregulation, pro-growth policies, and decisive enforcement, the Trump
Administration is slashing waste, protecting American workers, and
stacking victory after victory.

**Here are a few recent wins you may have missed:**

**🍎** **Food stamp dependency is plummeting.** More than [4.3
million](https://x.com/SecRollins/status/2050712694055928113)
[fewer](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/more-than-three-million-people-have-lost-federal-food-aid-061b0a28)
Americans are on food stamps since President Trump signed the landmark
Working Families Tax Cuts Act. Illegal aliens have been removed from the
rolls and requirements restored for able-bodied adults — safeguarding
benefits for Americans who truly need them.

**🎓** **Foreign student enrollment is collapsing.** New data reveals a
[20%
drop](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-11/us-colleges-report-20-drop-in-foreign-students-over-visa-clampdown)
last spring after the Trump Administration dramatically tightened
international visas. This [multi-year
decline](https://www.ft.com/content/7a4860bc-061c-40d0-bf3b-2385977b84d7?syn-25a6b1a6=1)
is freeing thousands of spots for American students at colleges and
universities across the country.

**📋** **Health insurers are slashing red tape.** UnitedHealthcare has
[eliminated](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/unitedhealthcare-cuts-30-percent-prior-authorization-requirements/?ftag=CNM-00-10aab7e&linkId=937788089)
prior authorization requirements for 30% of procedures — the latest
major insurer to act after commitments
[secured](https://www.hhs.gov/press-room/kennedy-oz-cms-secure-healthcare-industry-pledge-to-fix-prior-authorization-system.html)
by the Trump Administration. Overall prior authorizations at the
nation’s largest insurers have
[dropped](https://x.com/SecKennedy/status/2052098084184936768) 11% from
last year, sparing 6.5 million procedures from bureaucratic delays.

**📈** **Economic fundamentals are roaring with strength.** The U.S.
economy [added](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm) 172,000
jobs in May, crushing expectations. Manufacturing and construction are
[booming](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/pdf/empsit.pdf), government
employment has
[fallen](https://x.com/RealEJAntoni/status/2062887398619463705) for 17
straight months, small business hiring is
[up](https://www.paychex.com/newsroom/news-releases/small-business-employment-watch-may-2026)
for the third month in a row, and job openings are
[near](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/job-openings-april-2026.html) a
two-year high. Manufacturing has
[expanded](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-01/us-manufacturing-activity-expands-by-most-in-four-years)
for the fifth straight month at the fastest pace in four years. The
services sector has
[grown](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/services-pmi-at-54-5-may-2026-ism-services-pmi-report-302789082.html)
every month since President Trump’s return. New home construction is
[surging](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-29/us-housing-starts-surge-to-highest-level-since-december-2024/),
home prices are falling in over half the country, and income growth is
[outpacing](https://www.zillow.com/research/march-2026-rent-report-36269/)
rents —
[restoring](https://www.zillow.com/research/march-2026-rent-report-36269/#:~:text=The%20share%20of%20income%20the%20median%20household%20spends%20on%20typical%20rent%20has%20eased%20to%2026.5%25%2C%20down%20slightly%20from%20a%20year%20ago%20and%20nearing%20the%20pre%2Dpandemic%20level%20of%2025.8%25)
housing affordability to near pre-pandemic levels.

**🏠** **Immigration and visa enforcement are driving down housing
costs.** Aggressive crackdowns on [illegal
immigration](https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2026/01/mass-deportations-are-improving-americans-quality-of-life/)
and [H-1B](https://www.state.gov/h-1b-faq) [visa
abuse](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/09/restriction-on-entry-of-certain-nonimmigrant-workers/)
are
[slashing](https://nypost.com/2026/06/05/real-estate/trumps-crackdown-on-h1b-visa-abuse-sends-dallas-home-prices-down/)
housing demand (and
[costs](https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2026/01/mass-deportations-are-improving-americans-quality-of-life/#:~:text=In%2014%20of,the%20Trump%20Administration.))
across the country — especially in [high-immigration tech
hubs](https://nypost.com/2026/06/05/real-estate/trumps-crackdown-on-h1b-visa-abuse-sends-dallas-home-prices-down/)
— delivering real relief where costs had surged the most and helping
bring housing back within reach for American families.

**💊** **Drug prices are actually falling.** President Trump is the
first President to actually *[bring
down](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2056405481107677599?s=20)*
overall prescription drug prices, and the data is only starting to show
up — with President Trump’s landmark Most Favored Nation initiative
[projected](https://www.whitehouse.gov/research/2026/05/savings-from-most-favored-nation-drug-pricing-policy/)
to
[save](https://apnews.com/article/trump-prescription-drug-prices-3ff64b481fe42e6c54378710e07ef27a)
Americans $500 billion over the next decade while protecting innovation
and expanding access. Meanwhile, [TrumpRx](https://trumprx.gov/)
continues
[rapidly](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2061860658669384177)
adding discounted prescriptions — including
[hundreds](https://apnews.com/article/trump-prescription-drug-prices-3ff64b481fe42e6c54378710e07ef27a)
of new generic medications — to its platform, saving Americans hundreds
of millions of dollars already.

**🏗️** **Steel imports are collapsing as American production surges.**
Thanks to President Trump’s strong tariffs protecting U.S. steel and
aluminum, steel imports have dropped 30% this year while domestic mills
have
[added](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/steel-imports-down-30-percent-ytd-tariffs-bolster-us-production/821998/)
nearly five million tons of production — putting American steelworkers
first after years of foreign exploitation.
