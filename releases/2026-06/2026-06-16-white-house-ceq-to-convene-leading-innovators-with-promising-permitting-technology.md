title: White House CEQ to Convene Leading Innovators with Promising Permitting Technology
---
 
*CEQ invites industry to showcase solutions at the inaugural Permitting
Innovators Expo.*

Today, the <a href="https://permitting.innovation.gov/"
id="https://permitting.innovation.gov/">Council on Environmental
Quality</a>, in collaboration with NASA’s Center of Excellence for
Collaborative Innovation, opened the attendee
<a href="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/"
id="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/">interest form</a> for
the Permitting Innovators Expo — a full-day event focused on shaping the
future of environmental review and permitting through public-private
collaboration.

The <a href="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/"
id="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/">Permitting Innovators
Expo</a> will take place on July 31, 2026, at the Hilton Arlington
Rosslyn The Key. The event will feature high-potential solutions, each
with dedicated space to demonstrate their technologies to federal
agencies, policymakers, and industry leaders in environmental review and
permitting. Event programming will also include plenary and breakout
sessions, where speakers will share lessons learned and best practices
in modern federal permitting.

“President Trump took decisive <a
href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/updating-permitting-technology-for-the-21st-century/"
id="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/updating-permitting-technology-for-the-21st-century/">action</a>
to ensure the U.S. applies modern technology to longstanding problems in
Federal permitting. Now, the private-sector has the opportunity to play
a valuable role in providing solutions,” said **Council on Environmental
Quality Chairman Katherine Scarlett**. “By bringing together innovators
and government, the Permitting Innovators Expo will help identify
high-potential solutions that can accelerate the delivery of critical
infrastructure projects to the American people.”  

**Top-scoring solutions to be featured at Expo**

The Expo will give federal agency staff an opportunity to evaluate all
qualified solutions side by side, engage directly with the teams behind
them, and assess how technologies could support implementation of the
<a href="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/"
id="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/">Permitting
Technology</a>
<a href="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/"
id="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/"></a>
<a href="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/"
id="https://permitting.innovation.gov/resources/action-plan/">Action
Plan</a> as part of President Trump’s Memorandum on *Updating Permitting
Technology for the 21st Century*.

Solutions featured at the Expo will also be included in the Permitting
Innovators Solutions Catalog, which will be shared with federal agencies
in late 2026.

Earlier this year, CEQ launched the Permitting Innovators Call for
Solutions, which invited innovators to submit solutions that could help
modernize and accelerate federal environmental reviews and permitting.
CEQ will review eligible submissions and invite top-scoring applicants
to participate in the Expo.

**Interest form now open for prospective attendees**

Space is limited. Leaders focused on environmental review and permitting
from government, industry, and policy interested in attending the Expo
may <a href="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/"
id="https://www.permittinginnovators.com/expo/">submit an interest
form</a>.
