**As Radical Left Democrats force the Department of Homeland Security**
[**shutdown**](https://www.dhs.gov/news/2026/02/17/another-democrat-government-shutdown-dramatically-hurts-americas-national-security)
**into its 35th day, everyday Americans are bearing the cost.** More
than [100,000 DHS
employees](https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2026/03/democrats-reckless-dhs-shutdown-hits-americans-hard-as-100000-workers-go-without-pay/)
— including [50,000 TSA
officers](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/tsa-absences-double-shutdown-300-quit-airport-security-lines/#:~:text=The%20increase%20comes%20as%20roughly%2050%2C000%20transportation%20security%20officers%20are%20being%20required%20to%20work%20without%20pay%20during%20the%20DHS%20funding%20lapse%20that%20began%20Feb.%2014.)
— are working [without
paychecks](https://www.cbsnews.com/boston/news/government-shutdown-tsa-workers-logan-airport/)
for the third time in six months, leading to [skyrocketing
resignations](https://www.dhs.gov/news/2026/03/17/spring-break-under-siege-democrats-reckless-dhs-shutdown-forcing-tsa-officers-work)
and
[massive](https://www.atlantanewsfirst.com/2026/03/19/tsa-callout-rate-reaches-nearly-40-atlanta-airport-partial-shutdown-continues/)
[callouts](https://www.dhs.gov/news/2026/03/17/spring-break-under-siege-democrats-reckless-dhs-shutdown-forcing-tsa-officers-work#:~:text=On%20Sunday%2C%20March,gaps%20in%20staffing.)
that have crippled airports across the country.

**Americans are suffering from Democrats’ continued refusal to fund
DHS:**

-   **“I feel bad for the workers… They need to be paid!”**
    [said](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2034974061437079990)
    travelers at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, where
    [hours-long
    lines](https://x.com/Ella__Dorsey/status/2034953731322638680) snaked
    through the concourse after more than
    [one-third](https://www.atlantanewsfirst.com/video/2026/03/18/frustration-lines-grow-atlantas-hartsfield-jackson-airport/)
    of TSA officers called out.
-   **“We were going to go on 11, but we rebooked to 4:30… We got here
    three-and-a-half hours early,”**
    [said](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2035037550146945324) a
    frustrated traveler at George Bush Intercontinental Airport in
    Houston, where lines
    [snaked](https://abc13.com/post/tsa-wait-times-houston-bush-airport-closes-checkpoints-partial-government-shutdown-drags/18740136/)
    down to the floor below due to staffing shortages.
-   **“Unreal. Longest \[TSA line\] I’ve been in — and I travel every
    week,”**
    [said](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2035047884295475462) a
    traveler at LaGuardia Airport in New York City, where even the TSA
    PreCheck line
    [stretched](https://ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/traffic_and_transit/2026/03/20/long-lines-persist-at-area-airports-as-partial-government-shutdown-continues)
    to the parking garage.
-   **“It’s so overwhelming… The TSA line was out the door around the
    building outside,”**
    [said](https://templeupdate.com/phl-airport-faces-inconsistent-wait-times-amid-shutdown/)
    a traveler at Philadelphia International Airport, where major
    checkpoints have been [shut
    down](https://templeupdate.com/phl-airport-faces-inconsistent-wait-times-amid-shutdown/#:~:text=To%20manage%20staffing%20shortages%2C%20Philadelphia%20International%20Airport%20has%20consolidated%20operations%2C%20closing%20several%20terminals.%20Terminals%20A%2DWest%20and%20F%20are%20currently%20shut%20down%2C%20joining%20Terminal%20C%2C%20which%20closed%20last%20week.)
    due to ongoing staffing shortages.
