### **President Trump took action where Democrats would not:**

-   **Emergency action to pay TSA officers:** President Trump
    [declared](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/03/memorandum-for-the-secretary-of-homeland-security-and-the-director-of-the-office-of-management-and-budget/)
    a national emergency to
    [pay](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2037296427538256274) TSA
    officers, with their [first
    paychecks](https://x.com/GMA/status/2038574206510645703) in weeks
    [reaching](https://x.com/RapidResponse47/status/2038605255697420459)
    workers as early as today, already reducing absenteeism and
    improving staffing levels.
-   **ICE support at airports:** To ease the burden on TSA personnel,
    President Trump
    [deployed](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/23/travel/ice-agents-tsa-airports.html)
    ICE officers to major airports, where they’re assisting with crowd
    control, logistics, entrance and exit security, and identity
    verification — helping maintain order and keep travelers moving
    safely.

### **Media outlets are already reporting measurable improvements:**

-   [**CNN**](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/30/us/tsa-airports-back-pay-wait-times)**:**
    “Abysmal wait times at airports shrank Monday morning as tens of
    thousands of Transportation Security Administration workers started
    getting back pay after more than a month without income — apparently
    prompting fewer to call out.”
-   [**Associated
    Press**](https://apnews.com/article/airport-travel-delays-tsa-trump-b2dcb2933f62751b6cc13e7d4e2a68d6)**:**
    “After weeks of chaos in U.S. airports, the Transportation Safety
    Administration said the first paychecks in weeks are being sent as
    early as Monday to its workers, giving the beleaguered aviation
    system a boost of optimism. Wait times at some TSA security
    bottlenecks, such as the airport checkpoints in Atlanta and Houston,
    improved significantly Monday morning.”
-   [**NOLA.com**](https://www.nola.com/news/tsa-msy-lines-update/article_b3378152-d27e-4d97-96ea-108e7df28c7b.html)**:**
    “Early morning travelers reported another round of long lines at
    Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport on Monday, but, as
    on Sunday, lines moved faster than the ones that had snarled the
    airport in previous weeks.”
-   [**WBAL-TV (Baltimore,
    MD)**](https://www.wbaltv.com/article/ice-at-bwi-marshall-security-line-wait-times-subside/70876266)**:**
    “After a congested and contentious spring break weekend, with some
    passengers stuck in queues for four or five hours, security wait
    times at BWI Marshall Airport have stabilized going into the
    workweek.”
