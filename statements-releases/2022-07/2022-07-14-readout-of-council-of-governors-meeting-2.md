On Wednesday, July 13, senior officials from the White House, Department
of Defense, and Department of Homeland Security participated in the
25<sup>th</sup> plenary meeting of the Council of Governors and the
second plenary meeting under the Biden-Harris Administration to discuss
homeland defense and security priorities. Secretary of Homeland Security
Alejandro N. Mayorkas, Deputy Secretary of Defense Dr. Kath Hicks,
Assistant to the President Julie Chavez Rodriguez, and Special Assistant
to the President Caitlin Durkovich provided opening remarks along with
the Co-Chairs of the Council of Governors, Ohio Governor Mike DeWine and
Minnesota Governor Tim Walz. The Council of Governors members received
updates on cybersecurity, disaster preparedness, workforce development,
and support for military families.  The Council also received an
overview of the National Defense Strategy from Assistant Secretary
Melissa Dalton, an update on preparedness for the 2022 disaster season
from FEMA Administrator Criswell, and information on the strategic
guidelines for the Department of Defense Fiscal year 2024 program from
Under Secretary of Defense McCord.  Finally, Director Aylward provided
an overview of the progress made on the 50<sup>th</sup> Anniversary
Vietnam War commemoration efforts. 

  
Participants from the Council of Governors included:

-   Governor Tim Walz (MN), Co-Chair of the Council of Governors
-   Governor Mike DeWine (OH), Co-Chair of the Council of Governors
-   Governor John Carney (DE)
-   Governor John Bel Edwards (LA)
-   Governor Gretchen Whitmer (MI)
-   Governor Kate Brown (OR)
-   Governor Spencer Cox (UT)
-   Governor Phil Scott (VT)
-   Governor Mark Gordon (WY)
