NOMINATIONS SENT TO THE SENATE:

     Amanda Bennett, of the District of Columbia, to be Chief Executive
Officer of the United States Agency for Global Media, vice Michael Pack.

     Brandon B. Brown, of Louisiana, to be United States Attorney for
the Western District of Louisiana for the term of four years, vice David
C. Joseph, resigned.

     Ryan K. Buchanan, of Georgia, to be United States Attorney for the
Northern District of Georgia for the term of four years, vice Byung J.
Pak, resigned.

     Robert McKinnon Califf, of North Carolina, to be Commissioner of
Food and Drugs, Department of Health and Human Services, vice Stephen
Hahn.

     Marisa T. Darden, of Ohio, to be United States Attorney for the
Northern District of Ohio for the term of four years, vice Justin E.
Herdman, resigned.

     Jose Emilio Esteban, of California, to be Under Secretary of
Agriculture for Food Safety, vice Mindy Brashears.

     Sharon Bradford Franklin, of Maryland, to be Chairman and Member of
the Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board for the remainder of the
term expiring January 29, 2024, vice Adam I. Klein, resigning.

     Jason M. Frierson, of Nevada, to be United States Attorney for the
District of Nevada for the term of four years, vice Nicholas A.
Trutanich, resigned.

     Eddie M. Frizell, of Minnesota, to be United States Marshal for the
District of Minnesota for the term of four years, vice Ramona L. Dohman.

     Ronald C. Gathe, Jr., of Louisiana, to be United States Attorney
for the Middle District of Louisiana for the term of four years, vice
Brandon J. Fremin, resigned.

     Geeta Rao Gupta, of Virginia, to be Ambassador at Large for Global
Women’s Issues, vice Kelley Eckels Currie.

     Dawn N. Ison, of Michigan, to be United States Attorney for the
Eastern District of Michigan for the term of four years, vice Matthew J.
Schneider, resigned.

     Christopher Joseph Lowman, of Virginia, to be an Assistant
Secretary of Defense, vice William Jordan Gillis.

     Andrew M. Luger, of Minnesota, to be United States Attorney for the
District of Minnesota for the term of four years, vice Erica H.
MacDonald, resigned.

     LaDon A. Reynolds, of Illinois, to be United States Marshal for the
Northern District of Illinois for the term of four years, vice Edward L.
Gilmore, resigned.

     Elizabeth H. Richard, of Virginia, a Career Member of the Senior
Foreign Service, Class of Career Minister, to be Coordinator for
Counterterrorism, with the rank and status of Ambassador at Large, vice
Nathan Alexander Sales.

     Robert Phillip Storch, of the District of Columbia, to be Inspector
General, Department of Defense, vice Jon T. Rymer, resigned.

     Mark A. Totten, of Michigan, to be United States Attorney for the
Western District of Michigan for the term of four years, vice Patrick A.
Miles, Jr., resigned.

     Beth Ann Williams, of New Jersey, to be a Member of the Privacy and
Civil Liberties Oversight Board for the remainder of the term expiring
January 29, 2023, vice Jane Nitze, resigned.
