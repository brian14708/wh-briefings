---
 
President Joseph R. Biden, Jr. met with President Volodymyr Zelenskyy of
Ukraine today to discuss U.S. support for Ukraine in its efforts to
defend against Russian aggression. The leaders discussed the state of
the war and Ukraine’s urgent assistance needs. The President announced a
new package of security assistance for Ukraine, which is valued at $225
million and includes air defense interceptors, artillery ammunition, and
other critical capabilities. This is the sixth security assistance
package announced by the United States since President Biden signed the
national security supplemental, demonstrating the rapid pace of U.S.
assistance for Ukraine. The leaders discussed the upcoming Peace Summit
on Ukraine in Switzerland, where Vice President Harris and National
Security Advisor Sullivan will represent the United States in support of
Ukraine’s pursuit of a just and lasting peace in line with the UN
Charter, including the principles of sovereignty and territorial
integrity. The President emphasized that U.S. support for Ukraine is
unwavering. The leaders committed to meet again at the upcoming G7
Summit in Italy to continue their discussions.
