  Executive Officer of AmeriCorps

WASHINGTON – Today, President Joe Biden announced his intent to nominate
Michael D. Smith for Chief Executive Officer of AmeriCorps, formerly
known as the Corporation for National and Community Service, which works
to bring people together to tackle the country’s most pressing
challenges through national service and volunteerism.

**<u>Michael D. Smith, Nominee for Chief Executive Officer of the
Corporation for National and Community Service</u>**

Michael Smith currently serves as Executive Director of the My Brother’s
Keeper (MBK) Alliance and Director of Youth Opportunity Programs at the
Obama Foundation. MBK Alliance leads a national call to action to build
safe and supportive communities for boys and young men of color where
they are valued and have clear pathways to opportunity. Smith was part
of the team that designed and launched the My Brother’s Keeper
initiative in the Obama Administration, and was appointed Special
Assistant to the President and Senior Director of Cabinet Affairs for My
Brother’s Keeper, managing the initiative and interagency Task Force at
the White House. 

Prior to joining the White House team, Smith was director of the Social
Innovation Fund (SIF), Senior Vice President of Social Innovation at the
Case Foundation, and helped build national initiatives aimed at bridging
the “digital divide.” Smith is a Senior Atlantic Fellow for Racial
Equity and a member of Boys and Girls Clubs of America’s Alumni Hall of
Fame, the highest honor bestowed by the organization. He also serves on
the board of directors of Results for America and Venture Philanthropy
Partners.
