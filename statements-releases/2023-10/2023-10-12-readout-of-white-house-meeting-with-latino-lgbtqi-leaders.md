On Tuesday, the White House Office of Public Engagement convened over 70
Latino LGBTQI+ leaders, in celebration of Hispanic Heritage Month and
National Coming Out Day, to discuss priority issues and the Biden-Harris
Administration’s ongoing efforts to advance equity for LGBTQI+ and
Latino communities. 

Administration officials and community leaders discussed recent actions
President Biden has taken on issues including civil rights, community
safety, health, and education including recent Department of Justice
actions to combat hate-related violence, efforts to improve health care
outcomes for Latinos living with HIV/AIDS and the newly formed Office of
Gun Violence Prevention to enhance the President’s longstanding efforts
to eradicate gun violence and community-based crimes.

Community leaders shared personal anecdotes that underscored the
importance of creating community-centered spaces focused on solution
development and agreed to continue working with the Biden-Harris
Administration to support the Latino LGBTQI+ community.
