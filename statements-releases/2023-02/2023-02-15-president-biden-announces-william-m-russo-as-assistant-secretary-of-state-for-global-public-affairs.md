WASHINGTON – Today, President Joe Biden announced he has appointed
William M. Russo to serve as Assistant Secretary of State for Global
Public Affairs at the U.S. Department of State. 

**<u>William M. Russo, Appointee for Assistant Secretary of State for
Global Public Affairs, Department of State</u>**

William Russo is the Acting Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary of
State in the Bureau of Global Public Affairs. Prior to that, he served
as the Bureau’s Deputy Assistant Secretary for Strategic Messaging from
January 2021 until April 2022. Russo previously served at the Department
of State as Advisor to then-Deputy Secretary Antony Blinken from
2015-2017. Russo formerly served as the Deputy Communications Director
for Press on the Biden-Harris campaign. Earlier, he served as the
Spokesperson for President Biden at the conclusion of his vice
presidency in the Obama-Biden Administration. Russo began his time in
government service in the Office of then-Vice President Joe Biden,
working in multiple roles including Senior Advisor to then-National
Security Advisor to the Vice President, Jake Sullivan, as well as on the
National Security Council. Originally from Exton, Pennsylvania, Russo
holds an M.A. in Environment, Development, and Policy from the
University of Sussex in Brighton, England, and an Honors B.A. in
Political Science, History, and English from the University of Delaware.
