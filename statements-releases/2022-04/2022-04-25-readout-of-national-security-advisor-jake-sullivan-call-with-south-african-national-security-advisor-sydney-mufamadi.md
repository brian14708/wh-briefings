National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan spoke today with South African
National Security Advisor Sydney Mufamadi. Mr. Sullivan and Mr. Mufamadi
reaffirmed President Biden and President Ramaphosa’s commitment to
strengthen the U.S-South Africa bilateral relationship. Mr. Sullivan
pledged support to the people and government of South Africa in response
to the deadly floods in Kwa Zulu Natal. He also emphasized the United
States’ continued readiness to work closely with South Africa to address
the crisis in Ukraine; enhance our partnership on trade and investment,
health systems and health security, and climate change; and support
counterterrorism and regional security efforts. They highlighted the
need for an immediate end to Russia’s aggression in Ukraine, and shared
views on addressing the impact on supply chains, commodity prices, and
food security in Africa.
