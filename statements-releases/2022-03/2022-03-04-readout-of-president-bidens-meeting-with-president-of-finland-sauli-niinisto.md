President Joseph R. Biden, Jr. met today with President of Finland Sauli
Niinistö to discuss strengthening bilateral relations and ensuring
strong defense and deterrence in northern Europe. The leaders also
discussed ongoing transatlantic coordination to impose costs on Russia
for its unprovoked and unjustified military invasion of Ukraine, and
their respective efforts to provide security and humanitarian assistance
to Ukraine. The Presidents committed to start a process that would
strengthen U.S.-Finnish security cooperation, which would be conducted
in close consultation with other Nordic countries. The Presidents also
discussed the importance of NATO’s Open Door policy. In addition, they
discussed energy security and efforts to address climate change.
