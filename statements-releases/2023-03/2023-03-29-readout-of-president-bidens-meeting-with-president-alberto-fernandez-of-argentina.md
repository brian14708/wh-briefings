President Joseph R. Biden, Jr. met today with President Alberto
Fernández of Argentina at the White House. The two leaders discussed the
second Summit for Democracy currently underway, and committed to
cooperate to strengthen democratic institutions and good governance
practices in the region. President Biden and President Fernández also
discussed cooperation between the United States and Argentina to
continue supporting the people of Ukraine and to respond to the
consequences of Russia’s brutal invasion, including on food security.  
   
President Biden commended President Fernández for his work to tackle
climate change, and expressed his support for continued cooperation in
the energy and critical minerals sectors. President Biden and President
Fernández also committed to deepening U.S.-Argentina cooperation on
leading edge technology as well as working together with other G20
countries on reforming multilateral development banks to better address
global challenges.
