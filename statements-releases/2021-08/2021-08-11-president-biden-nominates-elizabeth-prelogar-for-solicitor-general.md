WASHINGTON – Today, President Joe Biden announced he has
nominated Elizabeth Prelogar as Solicitor General at the Department
of Justice.  
  
**<span style="text-decoration: underline">Elizabeth Prelogar, Nominee
for Solicitor General, Department of Justice  
</span>**  
Elizabeth Prelogar began serving as the Acting Solicitor General at the
U. S. Department of Justice in January 2021. Prelogar has focused her
career on Supreme Court and appellate litigation. From 2014 to 2019, she
served as an Assistant to the Solicitor General.  During her prior
tenure at the Department of Justice, Prelogar was asked to serve on the
Special Counsel’s Russia investigation and was an Assistant Special
Counsel to Robert S. Mueller III. Prelogar has also spent time as a
partner at Cooley LLP and an associate at Hogan Lovells, and she taught
a course at Harvard Law School on Supreme Court and appellate
advocacy.   
  
After graduating from Harvard Law School, Prelogar clerked for Judge
Merrick B. Garland of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit.
She then completed consecutive Supreme Court clerkships for Justice Ruth
Bader Ginsburg and Justice Elena Kagan. Prelogar previously completed a
master’s degree in creative writing at the University of St. Andrews in
Scotland, earned her undergraduate degree in English and Russian from
Emory University, and was a Fulbright Fellow in St. Petersburg, Russia.
Born and raised in Idaho, Prelogar lives in Washington, D.C. with her
husband and their two sons.
