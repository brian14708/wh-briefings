**EXPANDING AMERICANS’ FREEDOM TO FIX THEIR OWN VEHICLES: **Today,
President Donald J. Trump signed a Presidential Memorandum expanding
Americans’ freedom to fix their own vehicles.

- The Memorandum directs the Administrator of the Environmental
  Protection Agency (EPA) to issue guidance clarifying what actions
  individuals may take to fix their own vehicles’ emissions systems,
  consistent with the Clean Air Act. 
- The Memorandum directs the EPA Administrator to encourage and expedite
  alternative certification pathways for aftermarket parts, reducing
  reliance on California’s slow and costly certification process and
  providing greater certainty to manufacturers and consumers.
- The Memorandum instructs the EPA Administrator to consider
  deprioritizing civil enforcement actions against individuals who, in
  good faith, attempt to fix their own vehicles to their original
  configuration. 

**REDUCING REGULATORY BARRIERS TO AFFORDABLE VEHICLE
REPAIRS:** President Trump is lowering costs for American families by
cutting burdensome regulations and expanding affordable vehicle repair
options. 

- The Biden Administration’s crushing environmental regulatory burdens
  caused the average cost of vehicles to soar, squeezing American
  families both at the dealership and in the repair shop.
- The only certification process currently recognized under the Clean
  Air Act is run by the California Air Resources Board (CARB) – a
  process that now routinely takes well over a year to complete, even
  when all paperwork and testing are in order.
- Because CARB’s process is effectively the only accepted certification
  pathway, the supply of compliant aftermarket parts has been
  bottlenecked by a single state agency, driving up costs, limiting
  supply, and reducing consumer choice.

**DELIVERING HISTORIC DEREGULATION TO LOWER COSTS FOR
AMERICANS:** President Trump has taken unprecedented action to remove
burdensome regulations, restore consumer choice, and lower costs for
American families. 

- In February 2026, President Trump’s EPA released guidance affirming
  farmers’ ability to lawfully fix their own agricultural and non-road
  equipment, saving an estimated $33,000 per repair on average.
- In February 2026, President Trump announced the single largest
  deregulatory action in American history: the full revocation of the
  disastrous Obama-era “Endangerment Finding” and the consumer mandates
  that depended on it.
- In December 2025, President Trump delivered major relief to American
  families by resetting the Biden Administration’s costly and unlawful
  Corporate Average Fuel Economy (CAFE) standards to levels that can
  actually be met with conventional gasoline and diesel vehicles.
- In July 2025, President Trump signed into law the Working Families Tax
  Cuts Act, which set the civil penalty for violating CAFE standards to
  $0, protecting the U.S. auto manufacturing industry from significant
  fines.
- In June 2025, President Trump signed a joint resolution to end the
  California EV mandates, which would have effectively been a 100% ban
  on new gas cars sold in the state by 2035 (with similar effects in 17
  states that adopted California’s standards).
